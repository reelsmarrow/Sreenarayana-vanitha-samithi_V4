    About Us

    Sree Narayana Vanitha Samithy Kollam was established in 1990 by a group of philanthropic women to provide services for women and the community based on the ideals and exhortations of Sree Narayana Guru.

    The objectives include

    Samithy provides social and cultural services in keeping with the goals set at the time of its inception.

    • To learn and propagate the principles and teachings of Sree Narayana Guru by conducting seminars and discourses for women.
    • To conduct social service programmes for women and children.
    • To empower women in all fields.
    • To conduct value based educational programmes like premarital counselling and career counselling
    • To conduct medical camps.
    • To achieve the above mentioned goals ,the Samithy has set up two working groups as the different arms of the organization.
      1.Pakalveedu
      2.SNISH

    Pakalveedu

    "Pakalveedu," initiated by the Sree Narayana Vanitha Samithy in October 2008, offers compassionate care, support, and companionship to women aged 50 and above, all while allowing them to remain within the comfort and security of their own homes during the day.

    At Pakalveedu, these women have the opportunity to socialize with peers in their age group, fostering a sense of camaraderie and community. The day begins with a prayer, followed by a range of activities aimed at promoting mental alertness, physical well-being, and social engagement. These activities include yoga sessions, simple games, discussions, regular medical check-ups by Samithy doctors, informative medical talks, and opportunities to showcase cultural talents through performances. Additionally, Pakalveedu organizes picnics and outings to provide enriching experiences beyond the facility's walls. Nutritious lunch and refreshments are provided, and transportation to and from Pakalveedu is arranged by the Samithy, ensuring the women's safety and convenience. Located at Sree Narayana Bhavanam in Kadappakkada, Kollam, Pakalveedu embodies the Samithy's commitment to enhancing the quality of life for elderly women while preserving their dignity and autonomy within their familial environment.

    SNISH-Sree Narayana Institute of Speech and Hearing

    SNISH (Sree Narayana Institute of Speech and Hearing) is a beacon of hope and support for differently-abled children, operating under the compassionate umbrella of Sree Narayana Vanitha Samithy

    Established in 2010, SNISH is dedicated to providing specialized education and care to children with disabilities. The school boasts a team of trained educators with expertise in special education and registered with the Rehabilitation Council of India (RCI). Additionally, SNISH is equipped with a licensed clinical psychologist who offers invaluable behavioral therapy, helping students navigate their unique challenges and thrive. Importantly, SNISH is committed to inclusivity and social equity, offering free therapy to students from economically disadvantaged backgrounds. Moreover, recognizing the critical role of mothers in the holistic development of their children, SNISH extends empowerment opportunities to them through complimentary training in tailoring, embroidery, and crafting paper and fabric carry-bags. These initiatives not only uplift families but also foster a sense of community and self-sufficiency. Situated within the welcoming confines of Sree Narayana Bhavanam, SNISH embodies the ethos of compassion, inclusion, and empowerment championed by the Sree Narayana Vanitha Samithy, thereby enriching the lives of differently-abled children and their families.
